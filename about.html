About the Volta River Authority

The Volta River Authority (VRA) is Ghana’s leading energy producer, tasked with generating and supplying reliable electricity through hydro, thermal, and renewable means. Established in 1961, VRA has played a pivotal role in the industrial development of Ghana and continues to drive the country’s transition to sustainable energy.

Our History

The Volta River Authority was established following the passage of the Volta River Development Act, 1961 (Act 46), to manage the development of the Akosombo Hydroelectric Project. This project led to the construction of the Akosombo Dam and the creation of Lake Volta — one of the largest man-made lakes in the world.

Over the decades, VRA expanded into thermal energy, renewables, and transnational electricity trade, evolving into a comprehensive energy utility serving Ghana and neighboring countries.

Our Mission & Vision

Mission

To provide efficient and reliable electricity generation and supply, while promoting sustainable development and environmental stewardship in Ghana and the West African region.

Vision

To be a model public power utility in Africa, pioneering innovative and sustainable energy solutions that empower people and transform economies.

Our Core Values

  • Integrity: We act ethically and responsibly in all we do.
  • Excellence: We strive for the highest quality in service delivery.
  • Innovation: We embrace new ideas and technologies.
  • Environmental Sustainability: We protect and preserve natural resources.
  • Social Responsibility: We contribute to the well-being of our communities.

Community & Sustainability

VRA supports education, health, and environmental initiatives across Ghana. We have constructed schools and clinics in underserved communities, provided scholarships for students, and supported resettlement programs near our power projects.

Our commitment to sustainability includes watershed protection, biodiversity conservation, and renewable energy expansion to reduce Ghana’s carbon footprint.
