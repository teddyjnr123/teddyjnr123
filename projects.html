Our Energy Projects

The Volta River Authority oversees a diverse portfolio of energy generation projects — from large hydroelectric dams to innovative renewable energy initiatives — providing reliable power to Ghana and the region.

Our infrastructure includes world-class hydro, thermal, and solar facilities, continuously expanded and upgraded to meet rising energy demands sustainably.

Hydroelectric Power Plants

Akosombo Hydroelectric Plant

Commissioned in 1965, the Akosombo Dam is Ghana’s largest hydroelectric power station with a capacity of 1,020 MW. It created Lake Volta, the largest man-made lake in the world, which supports power generation, irrigation, and transportation.

  • Capacity: 1,020 MW
  • Location: Eastern Region, Ghana
  • Significance: Backbone of Ghana’s power grid

Kpong Hydroelectric Plant

Located downstream from Akosombo, the Kpong plant was commissioned in 1982 and provides an additional 160 MW of clean energy. It plays a crucial role in grid stability and supports irrigation schemes.

  • Capacity: 160 MW
  • Location: Eastern Region, Ghana
  • Commissioned: 1982

Thermal Power Stations

Takoradi Thermal Power Station (TAPCO)

The TAPCO plant, located at Aboadze, supplies 330 MW of electricity to Ghana’s grid. It primarily runs on natural gas and supports industrial power demands on the coast.

  • Capacity: 330 MW
  • Fuel: Natural gas
  • Location: Aboadze, Ghana

Kpone Thermal Power Plant

This 230 MW plant uses modern combined cycle technology to provide efficient power to Tema’s industrial area, improving reliability and economic productivity.

  • Capacity: 230 MW
  • Technology: Combined cycle
  • Location: Tema, Ghana

Renewable Energy Projects

Navrongo Solar Plant

Ghana’s first grid-connected solar plant with 2.5 MW capacity, located in the Upper East Region. It contributes clean solar power and serves as a model for rural energy solutions.

  • Capacity: 2.5 MW
  • Location: Navrongo, Ghana
  • Commissioned: 2013

Lawra and Kaleo Solar Farms

Combined 13 MW solar farms supplying power to remote communities in Upper West Region, reducing dependence on fossil fuels and boosting local economies.

  • Capacity: 13 MW (combined)
  • Locations: Lawra and Kaleo, Ghana

Future Projects and Innovations

The Volta River Authority is investing heavily in smart grid technology, energy storage solutions, and expanding its renewable energy footprint. Future projects include the Pwalugu Multipurpose Dam and exploratory wind power farms.

Our commitment is to provide clean, affordable, and reliable electricity to all Ghanaians while supporting the nation’s economic growth and environmental sustainability goals.
